How it all started

As a child, I had a hobby of collecting spoiled electronics and trying to make something out of them. I had a near-death encounter with electrocution at the age of 3, but that only sparked (pun intended) my interest in electronics. I always sought to understand things deeply; with a resilient and keen mind toward learning. At the age of 12, I embarked on a journey of self-taught programming. I started out with the C programming language, and right off the bat, I wanted to make my own operating system. Despite lacking formal education in the field of programming until my second year of university, where I was introduced to the basics of C++, I had already delved into x86 assembly programming by the age of 15. I never settle because I believe there's always more to learn.
